Explore the wonders of El Nido, Coron, and Puerto Princesa

El Nido

El Nido is famous for its white-sand beaches, limestone cliffs, and crystal-clear waters. A paradise for island-hopping!

El Nido, located on the northern tip of Palawan, is an iconic tropical destination famous for its dramatic karst limestone cliffs rising from crystal-clear turquoise waters and pristine white-sand beaches. Visitors can enjoy island-hopping tours that showcase hidden lagoons, secret beaches, and vibrant coral reefs perfect for snorkeling and diving. Popular spots include Big Lagoon, Small Lagoon, and Secret Beach. El Nido’s charm lies not only in its scenery but also in its eco-friendly resorts and relaxing island lifestyle.

Coron

Coron is known for its turquoise lagoons, shipwreck dives, and scenic limestone landscapes — a must-visit for adventurers!

Coron, located on Busuanga Island, is a paradise for divers and nature enthusiasts. It’s home to stunning limestone formations, freshwater lakes like Kayangan Lake, and numerous World War II shipwrecks that attract scuba divers from around the world. Visitors can also hike Mt. Tapyas for panoramic views of the town and nearby islands, or relax in the Maquinit Hot Springs after a day of adventure. Coron offers a balance between thrilling exploration and serene natural beauty.

Puerto Princesa

Home to the Underground River, Puerto Princesa offers natural wonders, eco-tours, and tropical charm.

As the capital of Palawan, Puerto Princesa is the main gateway to the province’s natural wonders. Its most famous attraction, the Puerto Princesa Subterranean River National Park, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New 7 Wonders of Nature. Visitors can enjoy eco-tours, firefly watching along the Iwahig River, and pristine beaches such as Honda Bay. Puerto Princesa combines nature, adventure, and local hospitality in one unforgettable destination.
